Project Overview 
Students often struggle to transform stacks of lecture notes, slides and readings into a coherent 
and organised structure. Traditional study tools summarize information, but they do not show 
how concepts are connected to each other. This level of understanding comes from seeing the 
big picture and the connections between concepts. 
Our group project proposes MindMapAI, a learning assistant that uses Retrieval-Augmented 
Generation (RAG) technology. This assistant automatically generates mind maps from uploaded 
course materials. Students can upload documents such as lecture slides, PDFs and notes. 
With a RAG implementation, the assistant extracts relevant information from the uploaded 
documents and generates organised mind maps that organise important topics, subtopics and 
their connections. 
The aim is to enhance students’ knowledge organisation, concept understanding and exam 
preparation by transforming static knowledge into an interactive conceptual map. 
 
Project Objectives 
1. To develop a system that uses a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) model to 
retrieve information from the content of the courses. 
2. To automatically identify and retrieve the fundamental concepts and their relationships 
with one another. 
3. To have clear hierarchical representation of knowledge in the mind maps. 
4. To create a user interface that allows students to upload their materials and generate 
mind maps. 
5. To use prompt engineering and retrieval techniques to efficiently generate accurate 
outputs.
